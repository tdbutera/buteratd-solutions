## Why the "cheapest/safest" states didn't make it

The livability sweep's favorites — **Idaho, Wyoming, West Virginia, Mississippi, Montana** — are cheap, safe, low-disaster, and homeschool-friendly. They were **excluded because they have no meaningful cleared-cyber job market.** For this family the civilian salary ($137–180K) is the binding requirement, and you can't earn a cleared TS/SCI cyber wage where there are no cleared employers. **They re-enter instantly if your income becomes remote/portable** (see the pivot below).

Also filtered out:
- **Washington** — its vet property exemption is income-capped (~$40K); a $150K+ earner is disqualified.
- **California / DC** — fully tax both pension *and* salary (CA top 13.3%).
- **Maryland / Colorado** — best-in-class job markets, but high tax + high cost knock them to the runner-up tier.

## The one pivot that reorders everything: is your income portable?

The default weights put **22% on the local cyber job market** because that's where the $150–180K lives. **If your civilian income is remote or location-independent** (ButeraNet MSP, remote ISSM/consulting), drop the job-market weight toward zero and the ranking flips toward **cheap + safe + free + no-tax**:

- **Tennessee, Texas, and Utah** rise; **Alabama** stays strong; **Virginia and Maryland fall** (you'd be paying their income tax for a job market you no longer need).
- In that world a pure-livability pick like **Idaho or Wyoming** becomes viable.
