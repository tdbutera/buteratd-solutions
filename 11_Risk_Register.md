## The plan's load-bearing assumption (attack it first)

**Everything hinges on 100% P&T, and it is NOT guaranteed.** The headline numbers — $7,632/mo net, the $339,984 education lever, $0 property tax in TX/FL/VA/AL — all assume a **permanent AND total** rating. If it lands at 70–90%:
- Chapter 35 → **$0** (the single biggest swing evaporates).
- Full state property exemption → gone (you'd owe full property tax everywhere).
- Net drops to $5,223/mo.
- **The entire relocation calculus shifts** (property axis collapses; no-income-tax states matter even more).
> **This is the #1 risk. The plan is currently written as if P&T is locked. It isn't.** Mitigation: BDD claim with airtight evidence + nexus letters; identify secondary conditions and the TDIU path; do NOT make irreversible (home-purchase) decisions assuming P&T until the rating is in hand.

## Attacks on the relocation conclusion

1. **The flat-salary model flatters the cheap states.** Holding $150K constant across all 12 states is great for isolating tax/COL — but it *hides* that NoVA/Maryland realistically pay $175–200K while Knoxville/Huntsville may cap lower. Tennessee "winning" take-home at $150K is partly an artifact of assuming it pays the same as NoVA. **Real take-home gap is narrower than the table implies.**
2. **Salary bands are Tier-2.** The cleared-cyber pay ranges driving the job-market axis and the legend are industry-survey estimates, not BLS. BLS *metro* percentiles were never obtained (environment-blocked). The job-market scores could be off by a full point.
3. **1–10 scores are my judgment.** Positions 4–7 (UT/FL/TN/MD) sit within ~0.5 pts — noise. Small score changes reorder them. The top (TX) is robust; the middle is not.
4. **Single-source COL/insurance/crime.** BEA RPP, Bankrate, FBI — each one source, mostly state-level. Intra-state variance is huge (NoVA vs rural VA; Memphis vs Knoxville). **State numbers can mislead at the metro/county level.**
5. **Insurance is volatile and understated.** FL/TX/CO premiums are climbing double-digits yearly; today's figure may be low by the time you buy. FL's "stabilizing" market could re-break.
6. **TN cap unresolved.** $175K operative vs $250K proposed — and a partial-exemption state's whole property story depends on it.

## What would make this advice wrong
- Rating comes in below 100% P&T.
- Your civilian income is portable (then chasing a local job market is pointless — optimize purely for tax/COL/safety, and the ranking changes).
- You buy a high-value home (the cheap-state property-tax edge shrinks; the calculator assumes ~mid-priced metro homes).
- A target state changes its veteran exemption or income-tax law (several are mid-reform: TN cap, VA subtraction, state rate cuts).
