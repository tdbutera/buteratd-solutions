## Lifetime view (illustrative)

- **Comp:** child add-ons phase out at 18, but the **spouse-base delta persists for life** — 100% $4,158/mo vs 70% $1,961/mo = **~$2,197/mo (~$26.4K/yr) forever.**
- **Rough 100%-vs-70% advantage:** ~10 yr of comp delta + full Ch.35 ≈ **$629,000**, before counting state property-tax savings. Over a normal retirement lifespan the comp delta alone runs past **$650K**.

## What this means

1. **100% P&T is the highest-value financial objective in the entire plan** — bigger than relocation, PPM, or job choice. The education lever for 6 kids ($340K) exists *only* at P&T.
2. **It also unlocks the full state property-tax exemption**, which is what makes the relocation math (TX/FL/VA/AL) land at $0 property tax.
3. **The two are linked:** if the rating comes in at 70%, re-run relocation — the property axis compresses and the no-income-tax states matter even more.

## Action — pursue and document the rating

- Sequence the claim and **nexus letters** now; target a **Benefits Delivery at Discharge (BDD)** claim (180–90 days pre-separation) so the rating is adjudicated near retirement.
- Ensure the **P&T determination** specifically (not just 100% combined) — it's what gates Ch.35 and full property relief.
- If initially rated 70–90%, identify the path to 100% (secondary conditions, TDIU/individual unemployability) and track in `11_Risk_Register` / `12_Action_Tracker`.
